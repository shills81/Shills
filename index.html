The Work

Energy does not disappear.
It transforms.

VEXA is a volumetric smoke portrait series that gives form to what most art leaves invisible: the interior life of energy itself. Each piece is built from hand-made smoke compositions used to train AI and generate exquisite pieces, transformed through a rigorous visual process that preserves the character of the original material while revealing the emotional architecture beneath.

Five thousand years of energy measurement data inform the series. The work is not symbolic. It is structural. VEXA speaks in the language that energy has always spoken, and asks the viewer to listen with their body before their mind.

01 What is digital art?

Art made with software instead of a paintbrush

Digital art uses computers, cameras, and software as the creative tool. The result can be displayed on screens, printed at any scale, or exist as a unique file. It is as real as any canvas; the medium is simply different.

02 What is AI doing here?

AI was trained on the artist's own layered smoke work, not text prompts

VEXA pieces begin as real smoke compositions layered by hand in Adobe. That work was used to train an AI model, which then acts as a collaborator: transforming the smoke into portraits under the artist's direction. The AI did not create these. It was taught to see through them.

03 How does ownership work?

45 pieces. Some are one of a kind. Some are limited editions.

Each VEXA piece is either a 1/1 (the only copy that will ever exist) or a limited edition, available in two forms: digital or printed and hand-signed by the artist. Printed editions are exclusive to the Lisbon exhibition. Every piece is recorded on the blockchain so ownership is permanent and verifiable.

04 Why Lisbon?

The NFC Summit is where art and technology meet

The NFC Lisbon Summit brings together artists, collectors, and technologists at the frontier of digital culture. VEXA debuts here because it belongs at that intersection; this is not art about technology. It is art that technology made possible.
